About Me:

I am a Ph.D. Candidate in Applied Economics and Management at Cornell University. My research interests include the economics of digitization, data privacy, and intellectual property. I use applied microeconomics and experimental methods to study how individuals behave in information markets.

Prior to arriving at Cornell, I was an economic consultant for patent infringement cases in the U.S. telecommunications industry. I also have experience in strategy consulting for the consumer packaged foods industry. I received my B.A. in Economics from the University of Chicago in 2012.
