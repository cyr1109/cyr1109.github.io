Research



In general, I study what influences and motivates individual choices in information markets. I am especially interested in decision-making in data markets, where individuals supply personal data to firms that engage in secondary markets that exploit consumer data<. I am also interested in interaction of rights assignments and individual's information-sharing behavior, especially for intermediate inputs in producing final, commercial goods.

My primarily use randomized, controlled experiments to (1) exogenously manipulate individuals' frame of reference and information provisions and (2) elicit their willingness-to-pay or willingness-to-accept for real outcomes.
